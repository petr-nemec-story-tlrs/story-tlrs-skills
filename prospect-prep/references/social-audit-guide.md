# Social Media Audit Guide — Prospect Prep

> This guide defines HOW to conduct the social media audit for a prospect.
> The audit is the most valuable part of the prep for Story TLRS — a social media agency.
> Follow this guide step by step. Do not skip platforms. Do not skip fields.

---

## Why this matters

Story TLRS sells social media services. When Petr walks into a meeting and can say
"I looked at your Instagram — you post 3x a week but your engagement rate is 0.2%,
which is below industry average. Your last Reel got 5x more engagement than your statics —
have you considered shifting your content mix?" — that's what wins the deal.

Generic company research is table stakes. The social audit is the differentiator.

## How to find social profiles

1. **Check the company website** — look in footer, header, contact page for social icons/links
2. **Search directly:** `"[company name] instagram"`, `"[company name] facebook stránka"`, etc.
3. **Search on each platform** directly (Instagram search, Facebook page search, LinkedIn company search)
4. **Check Google:** `site:instagram.com "[company name]"`, `site:facebook.com "[company name]"`
5. **Cross-reference:** social profiles often link to each other in bio sections

## Platform-by-platform methodology

### Instagram (highest priority)

Search: `"[company name] instagram"` or go to instagram.com and search

**What to capture:**
- **Profile URL:** `https://www.instagram.com/[handle]/`
- **Followers:** number visible on profile
- **Following:** number (high following:follower ratio can indicate bought followers)
- **Posts count:** total posts
- **Posting frequency:** estimate from recent post dates (e.g., "~3 posts/week")
- **Content types:** What formats? Reels, carousels, single images, Stories highlights?
- **Content themes:** What topics? Product shots, behind-the-scenes, team, events, educational, promotional?
- **Visual style:** Consistent color palette? Professional photography? User-generated? Mix?
- **Tone of voice:** Read captions — formal/corporate, casual/friendly, expert, humorous?
- **Engagement:** Average likes and comments on recent 5-10 posts. Calculate rough engagement rate: (avg likes + comments) / followers × 100
- **Best performing:** Which posts got most engagement? What pattern?
- **Gaps:** Missing Reels? No Stories? Low engagement? Inconsistent posting? No CTAs? No community management (unanswered comments)?

### Facebook

Search: `"[company name] facebook"` or search on Facebook

**What to capture:**
- **Page URL**
- **Page likes / followers**
- **Posting frequency**
- **Content:** Same as Instagram or different? Cross-posted?
- **Community:** Do they have a Facebook Group? How active?
- **Reviews:** Star rating, number of reviews, sentiment
- **Response rate:** Does Facebook show "Usually responds within..."?
- **Engagement:** Likes, comments, shares on recent posts
- **Ads:** Check Facebook Ad Library (`facebook.com/ads/library`) — are they running ads? What kind?

### LinkedIn

Search: `"[company name] linkedin"` or search on LinkedIn

**What to capture:**
- **Company page URL**
- **Followers**
- **Employee count** (LinkedIn shows this)
- **Posting frequency**
- **Content themes:** Thought leadership, hiring, company culture, product updates?
- **Employee advocacy:** Do employees share/engage with company content?
- **Engagement level:** Likes, comments, reposts on recent posts
- **Tone:** Corporate/professional vs. human/casual

### TikTok

Search: `"[company name] tiktok"` or search on TikTok

**What to capture (if they have a profile):**
- **Profile URL**
- **Followers, likes**
- **Posting frequency**
- **Content style:** Trendy, educational, behind-the-scenes, product demos?
- **Video quality:** Professional production or phone-shot?
- **Engagement:** Views, likes, comments on recent videos

### YouTube

Search: `"[company name] youtube"` or search on YouTube

**What to capture (if they have a channel):**
- **Channel URL**
- **Subscribers**
- **Video count, posting frequency**
- **Content types:** Long-form, shorts, tutorials, testimonials, corporate videos?
- **View counts:** Average views on recent videos
- **Production quality**

### X/Twitter

Search: `"[company name] twitter"` or search on X

**What to capture (if active):**
- **Profile URL**
- **Followers**
- **Posting frequency**
- **Engagement**
- **Usage:** Customer service, news, thought leadership?

## Platforms NOT found

For each platform where you find NO presence, explicitly state:
> **[Platform]:** Not found / No active profile detected.

This is important information — a missing platform is a potential opportunity for Story TLRS.

---

## Social media summary

After auditing all platforms, write a summary paragraph (100-200 words) covering:

1. **Overall sophistication:** Rate as Beginner / Intermediate / Advanced
2. **Agency vs. in-house:** Any signs of agency involvement? (look for: tagged agency in posts, professional production quality, @agency mentions, consistent branded templates)
3. **Budget signal:** Low (phone photos, irregular), Medium (decent design, some video), High (professional production, paid media visible)
4. **Key strengths:** What they do well
5. **Key weaknesses:** What's clearly lacking
6. **Biggest opportunity:** The single most impactful thing Story TLRS could improve

## Benchmarks for context

These rough benchmarks help evaluate engagement quality:

| Platform | Good engagement rate | Low engagement rate |
|----------|---------------------|---------------------|
| Instagram | > 1% | < 0.5% |
| Facebook | > 0.5% | < 0.1% |
| LinkedIn | > 2% | < 0.5% |
| TikTok | > 3% | < 1% |

Engagement rate = (avg interactions per post / followers) × 100

Note: these are rough guidelines, actual benchmarks vary by industry and follower count.
